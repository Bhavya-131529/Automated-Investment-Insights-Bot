# Asset Allocation Strategies

Asset allocation is the process of dividing your investments among different asset categories, such as stocks, bonds, and cash.

## Core Strategies

### 1. Strategic Asset Allocation
This approach involves setting target allocations for various asset classes and rebalancing periodically to maintain those targets. It is based on the idea that the asset mix should reflect the investor's risk tolerance and time horizon.

### 2. Tactical Asset Allocation
This is a more active approach where investors occasionally deviate from their long-term target allocations to take advantage of market opportunities or mitigate risks.

### 3. Core-Satellite Allocation
This strategy combines a "core" of passive or low-cost investments (like index funds or ETFs) with "satellite" positions in more active or specialized investments (like individual stocks or sector-specific funds).

## Risk and Return
- **High Risk**: Higher allocation to stocks (equity). Suitable for long time horizons (10+ years).
- **Medium Risk**: A balance of stocks and bonds. Suitable for medium time horizons (5-10 years).
- **Low Risk**: Higher allocation to bonds and cash. Suitable for short time horizons (1-5 years).
