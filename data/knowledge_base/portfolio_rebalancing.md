# Portfolio Rebalancing and Insights

Portfolio rebalancing is the process of realigning the weightings of a portfolio of assets. It involves periodically buying or selling assets in a portfolio to maintain an original or desired level of asset allocation or risk.

## Why Rebalance?
- **Risk Control**: Over time, certain assets may grow faster than others, changing your initial risk profile. For example, if stocks do well, a 60/40 (Stocks/Bonds) portfolio might drift to 70/30, increasing risk.
- **Buy Low, Sell High**: Rebalancing forces you to sell assets that have appreciated (locking in gains) and buy assets that have underperformed (buying at a lower price).

## When to Rebalance
- **Time-Based**: Rebalancing on a set schedule, such as quarterly, semi-annually, or annually.
- **Threshold-Based**: Rebalancing when an asset class drifts from its target allocation by a predetermined percentage (e.g., +/- 5%).

## Analyzing Existing Holdings
When reviewing existing holdings, consider:
1. **Concentration Risk**: Are you too heavily invested in a single stock, sector, or country?
2. **Overlap**: Do you own multiple funds that track the same index or hold the same underlying assets?
3. **Alignment with Goals**: Does the current asset mix match your time horizon and risk tolerance? For example, a heavy stock allocation is not suitable for a short-term goal.
