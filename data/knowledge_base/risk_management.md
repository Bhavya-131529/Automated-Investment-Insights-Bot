# Diversification and Risk Management

Diversification is a technique that reduces risk by allocating investments across various financial instruments, industries, and other categories.

## Why Diversify?
- **Reduce Volatility**: Different assets often react differently to the same economic event.
- **Avoid Concentration Risk**: Not putting all your eggs in one basket prevents a single failure from destroying your portfolio.
- **Smoother Returns**: Over time, a diversified portfolio tends to have fewer extreme ups and downs.

## Diversification Rules
1. **Across Asset Classes**: Equities, Fixed Income, Real Estate, Commodities, Cash.
2. **Geographical**: Domestic and International markets.
3. **Sectoral**: Technology, Healthcare, Energy, Financials, etc.
4. **Time (Dollar Cost Averaging)**: Investing a fixed amount regularly (SIP) reduces the impact of market timing.

## Safety Warnings
- Past performance is not indicative of future results.
- Investments involve risk, including the loss of principal.
- Diversification does not guarantee a profit or protect against loss.
